## Features

- **Extreme compression** at the top of the compression slider, matching what online compressors achieve
    > GIFs get very lossy encoding above 80% and drop every 4th, 3rd or 2nd frame, shrinking a 20MB GIF to under 4MB. The *GIF frame dropping* setting in Settings > Images chooses what happens to timing: the animation plays faster with the remaining frames (default), or keeps its duration by showing each frame longer
    >
    > JPEG, PNG, WebP, HEIC and JXL push quality and palette size much lower than before; videos ramp to higher CRF values where each step roughly halves the size; PDFs at the lowest DPI settings also recompress embedded images harder
- **Fit under size**: get a file under a size limit in one action
    > Compression factors and encoder settings are tried first; the resolution is only lowered when the target can't be reached otherwise
    >
    > Assign the new action button to a result slot and press-drag it for a continuous size slider with magnetic stops (500 KB to 50 MB), or pick a preset from the *Fit under size* submenu in the result's right-click menu

## Fixes

- With the optimised file location set to *Same folder*, files dropped on Clop or picked up from watched folders no longer produce a doubled-suffix duplicate like `photo-optim-optim.png` next to `photo-optim.png`
    > The naming pattern was applied twice: the real optimised file got the doubled name, while the normally named copy was left unoptimised

## Improvements

- Floating results can also be dragged out when the drag starts on an empty `+` button slot; a plain click still opens the menu that assigns an action to the slot
    > To keep the press free for dragging, the menu opens when the mouse is released instead of when it is pressed
